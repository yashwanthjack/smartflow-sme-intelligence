Abstract— Small and medium-sized (SMEs) companies have 
proven economic competencies and great potential in light of the 
recent global economic transformations in developed and 
developing countries, which qualified them to play a major role 
in development and reform policy, by providing job 
opportunities, intensifying the industrial fabric, and supporting 
large industries. It should be noted that these institutions need 
greater efforts and important components that respond to their 
aspirations and idiosyncrasies, to be able to survive and compete 
in the markets, as we have witnessed the bankruptcy and 
collapse of many companies due to financial conditions and 
obstacles. In this paper, we proposed building a lending decision 
support system (DSS) for SMEs and researching the reasons 
that prevent them from lending, and what are the pillars on 
which the credit decision in banks depends, and how difficult it 
is to analyze the variables and their information, the data set 
represents SMEs lending requests from Jordan Ahli Bank. Four 
machine learning algorithms were used to build an automated 
system to support decision-making in granting credit. The 
classification accuracy of the models was evaluated where the 
accuracy exceeded 90%, and SVM outperformed other rating 
models. Our findings show that the challenges that SMEs face to 
obtain credit are summarized by the lack of the company's 
capital, the recent date of incorporation, and the lack of 
guarantees. 
Keywords— SME's, decision, decision support system, 
prediction, machine learning.

prediction results, but clean data and reasonable features can 
make the results much better [25]. Nowadays, more and more 
people understand the causes of many machine learning 
failures, not because of the quality of the algorithm, but 
because of data processing and feature selection [26]. 
Machine learning first needs to train the data, and each 
training data (or sample or example) can be considered as a 
vector of a set of feature sets [27].

selection, or variable subset selection. Indicates to select the 
optimal sub-feature set from the original practical feature set 
[28]. The model trained from the optimal feature subset 
obtained after screening has stronger predictive power [29]. 
The purpose of feature selection is to select the optimal 
feature subset.

[37]. This greatly reduces the time complexity of the sample, 
but in accuracy, through a large number of experiments, 
LightGBM has been used on some data sets and does not lose 
accuracy, sometimes even improving accuracy [37].

VII. CONCLUSION AND DISCUSSION 
Small and medium enterprises are no longer just an 
idea, but rather more than a reality. It is the experience of 
many countries that have come to believe that the 
development of their economies depends primarily on what 
these SMEs and industries achieve. It should be noted that 
these institutions need greater efforts and important 
components that respond to their aspirations and 
idiosyncrasies, to be able to survive and compete in the local 
and international markets, as many SMEs were recruited 
shortly before they collapsed or went bankrupt due to 
circumstances and bureaucratic obstacles and other financial 
obstacles, as Some of them remain facing these problems and 
obstacles. In this paper, we focused the research on the 
reasons that prevent SMEs from obtaining bank credit, what 
are the pillars on which the credit decision in banks depends, 
and how difficult it is to analyze the variables and information 
of the companies requesting credit. 
The trend was to search for information systems technologies 
that simulate human processes (thinking, creativity, intuition 
... etc.) to improve the effectiveness of decision-making and 
decision-making. Today's maker is faced with the challenge 
of data diversity and complexity, which carries many 
alternatives and hidden opportunities that may enable the 
organization to achieve a competitive advantage and strategy, 
but this phenomenon was accompanied by many challenges 
that made executives and decision-makers afraid to adopt it. 
To overcome these challenges, artificial intelligence, 
specifically machine learning, was used to help in making a 
mechanism for granting credit to the SME sector, due to its 
promising achievements and its tremendous capabilities in 
data analysis and access to accurate excerpts to come up with 
solutions and decisions that will help decision-makers in 
financial institutions, where we have built a DSS through 
artificial intelligence algorithms, which has achieved 
promising results in accurate analysis and prediction of 
decision helps financial institutions in taking credit decision 
without bias and automatically and orderly. Among the most 
important conclusions that we have found about the inability 
of SMEs to be unable to obtain credit, we summarize them as 
follows: 
1- Insufficient capital ratio of the company 
2- The increase in expenses compared to the profits achieved 
by the company 
3- Lack of sufficient guarantees in proportion to the required 
credit amount 
4- The recent date of establishment of the company 
5- The financial stability of the company in the local banking 
sector.
