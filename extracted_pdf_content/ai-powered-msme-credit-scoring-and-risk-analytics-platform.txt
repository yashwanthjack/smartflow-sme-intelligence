ABSTRACT

accuracy and efficiency of lending decisions. The study analyzes recent advancements in supervised learning, 
ensemble modeling, deep learning, and explainable AI (XAI) used in financial risk assessment. A systematic 
methodology was followed to examine models, datasets, evaluation metrics, and real -world deployment strategies. 
Findings indicate that algorithms such as Random Forest, XGBoost, and neura l networks outperform traditional 
statistical models in predicting MSME loan defaults, especially when enriched with alternative data sources like 
transaction records and GST data. The review further highlights the growing need for transparency in AI -driven credit

Keywords: Credit Scoring, Machine Learning, MSME Lending, Risk Analytics, Explainable AI, Prediction Models.  
1. INTRODUCTION 
 MSMEs contribute significantly to national economies by fostering innovation, generating employment, and 
supporting regional development. Despite their importance, these enterprises often struggle to obtain institutional 
credit due to the perceived high risk associated with limited financial records, lack of co llateral, and volatile cash 
flows. Traditional credit scoring models such as logistic regression and rule -based systems fail to capture the dynamic 
and non-linear nature of MSME financial behavior. 
 AI and ML provide advanced predictive capabilities by leve raging both structured and unstructured data, enabling 
lenders to evaluate MSME creditworthiness more accurately. Techniques such as Random Forest, Gradient Boosting, 
XGBoost, and neural networks outperform conventional scoring models by analyzing deep pat terns in borrower 
behavior, transaction history, and alternative digital footprints. 
 This review paper synthesizes the findings of recent studies (2020 –2025) focusing on AI -powered credit risk 
modeling for MSMEs. It evaluates the strengths and limitations of existing methodologies, highlights emerging 
innovations such as Explainable AI (XAI), graph neural networks (GNNs), and survival analysis models, and 
concludes by identifying future research directions. 
2. LITERATURE REVIEW 
2.1 Machine Learning Models in Credit Scoring 
Several studies demonstrate the superiority of ML models over traditional statistical techniques. Random Forest, 
XGBoost, and Support Vector Machines (SVM) consistently outperform logistic regression in predicting SME loan 
defaults. ML models  utilize ensemble learning and non -linear decision boundaries to capture complex borrower 
patterns. 
2.2 Use of Alternative Data 
Recent work highlights the importance of integrating bank statements, GST data, transaction logs, digital payments, 
and social m edia data for credit scoring. MSMEs often lack formal credit histories, and alternative data significantly 
improves prediction accuracy. 
2.3 Explainable AI (XAI) in Lending 
Financial institutions require transparency to comply with regulatory standards. XA I techniques such as SHAP and 
LIME provide model interpretability, allowing lenders to justify decisions and gain borrower trust.

2.4 Graph-Based Models and Network Analytics 
Graph attention networks (GAT) and relational graph neural networks (RGNNs) have been applied to model

3. METHODOLOGY 
3.1 Research Design 
The research follows a qualitative, analytical review framework. Studies related to machine learning, credit scoring, 
MSME lending, explainable AI, and risk modeling were identified using databases such as IEEE Xplore, Springer, 
ScienceDirect, MDPI, and Google Scholar. 
3.2 Data Collection Strategy 
A total of 50+ research articles, conference papers, and industry reports were collected based on relevance, recency, 
and technical depth. Keywords used included: “MSME credit scoring,” “machine learning credit risk,” “XAI in 
lending,” “SME risk prediction,” and “loan default modeling.” 
3.3 Screening and Inclusion Criteria 
Studies published between 2020 –2025, focusing on AI/ML credit scoring methods, were included. Articles not 
involving MSME datasets, outdated statistical models, or lacking experime ntal results were excluded. 
3.4 Analysis Method 
Each selected study was evaluated based on the following factors: 
1. Algorithms used (LR, RF, XGBoost, SVM, Neural Networks). 
2. Dataset type (financial statements, GST, bank transactions, alternative data).  
3. Techniques for preprocessing and feature engineering. 
4. Evaluation metrics such as Accuracy, AUC-ROC, F1-score. 
5. Integration of Explainable AI (SHAP/LIME). 
6. Strengths, limitations, and outcomes. 
3.5 Synthesis and Interpretation 
Findings from multiple studies were synthesized to compare the performance, interpretability, and real -world 
applicability of AI techniques. Patterns, gaps, and trends were identified to draw meaningful conclusions about the 
state of MSME credit scoring research. 
Most studies follow a structured pipeline: 
1. Data Collection: financial statements, bank transactions, credit history, GST, invoice data.  
2. Preprocessing: handling imbalance (SMOTE), feature engineering, normalization.  
3. Model Development: ML classifiers, ensemble models, deep learning networks. 
4. Explainability: SHAP, LIME, counterfactual explanations. 
5. Model Evaluation: accuracy, AUC-ROC, precision-recall, F1-score. 
4. MODELING AND ANALYSIS 
This section presents the modeling approaches and analytical techniques identified across existing research on AI -
powered MSME credit scoring and risk analytics. The analysis is aligned with the core concepts from the paper title, 
including AI-powered mode ling, MSME credit scoring , and risk analytics . The goal is to understand how different 
machine learning and deep learning models perform, how features are engineered, and how results are interpreted.  
4.1 Modeling Techniques 
A variety of AI/ML algorithms ha ve been employed in MSME credit scoring research. The most frequently used and 
high-performing models include: 
 Logistic Regression (LR): Serves as a baseline statistical model. 
 Random Forest (RF): Popular due to its robustness and ability to handle heterogeneous MSME data. 
 XGBoost and Gradient Boosting Models:  Deliver superior performance for imbalanced datasets and nonlinear 
relationships. 
 Support Vector Machines (SVM): Effective for high-dimensional credit data.

 Neural Networks (ANN, LSTM): Useful in capturing time-series trends and complex interactions.

4.2 Feature Engineering and Data Representation 
Studies highlight the importance of transforming raw MS ME financial and transactional data into predictive features. 
Common feature categories include: 
 Financial ratios (liquidity, profitability, leverage) 
 GST filings and compliance metrics 
 Bank transaction behaviors (cash flow patterns, credit utilization) 
 Repayment history and past loan behavior 
 Digital footprint and alternative behavioural data 
 Text-based features from business documents (when deep learning is used)  
4.3 Analysis of Model Performance 
Most reviewed studies use the following evaluation metrics: 
 Accuracy for overall prediction performance 
 AUC-ROC for distinguishing risky vs. safe borrowers 
 Precision & Recall for imbalanced MSME datasets 
 F1-score to balance false positives and false negatives 
Across studies, XGBoost and Random Forest consistentl y outperform traditional models , particularly when 
combined with advanced feature engineering and balanced training techniques like SMOTE.  
4.4 Explainability and Interpretation 
Given the regulatory need for transparent credit decisions, Explainable AI (XAI) techniques are widely used: 
 SHAP values: Identify the contribution of each feature to the credit risk prediction. 
 LIME: Generates local explanations for individual predictions. 
 Feature importance ranking: Used for financial interpretability. 
4.5 Analytical Insights 
Key observations from model analysis include: 
 Ensemble models provide the best balance of performance and interpretability.  
 Alternative data significantly improves MSME risk prediction. 
 Time-series models enhance early default detection. 
 XAI helps make AI models auditable and regulator-friendly. 
A comparison across 50+ reviewed papers shows: 
 XGBoost and Random Forest are the most widely used and consistent top performers. 
 Deep learning provides higher accuracy for large datasets but lacks interpretability. 
 XAI-integrated ML models strike a balance between performance and transparency. 
 Graph-based models are emerging but still under-explored for MSMEs. 
RESEARCH GAP 
 Limited availability of large, high-quality MSME datasets. 
 Insufficient research on fairness, bias detection, and ethical AI in credit scoring. 
 Need for more real-time credit scoring models using streaming financial data. 
 Lack of standardized evaluation metrics across studies.
